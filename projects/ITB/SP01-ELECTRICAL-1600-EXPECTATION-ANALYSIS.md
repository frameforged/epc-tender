## 1. OVERVIEW

The electrical discipline (Works Code 1600) of the SP01 package is a comprehensive work package with a 152-page Scope of Work document and 752 separate unit price items. This is not just cable pulling and panel installation — it is an "end-to-end" electrical construction and commissioning job ranging from the earthing network to high voltage switchgear, from heat tracing to cathodic protection, from lighting towers to SCADA testing.

**Key figures:**
- Total unit price items: **752**
- Cells to be filled by bidder: **442** (yellow cells — productivity and unit price)
- Work areas: **2 sites** (U58 + SS 00-BA-815 — two separate quantity columns)
- 7 main electrical systems + test/inspection system
- 30+ types of mandatory test equipment

### 5.3 Filling Rules

1. **Only yellow cells (columns J, L, M) are to be filled** — 442 cells
2. Do not modify formulas — automatic calculation is in place
3. **Column L:** Direct labour + direct material unit price (including scaffolding man-hours)
4. **Column M:** Second price component (if applicable)
5. **Column J:** Man-hours/unit productivity for each item (e.g.: 1 meter of cable pulling = X MH)
6. Total MH and USD must **exactly match** the Electrical Works column in PSSummary
7. Quantities are given separately for both sites (U58 and SS) but the **same unit price** will be applied

### 5.4 Meaning of Two Price Columns (L and M)

Per PD-1600:
- **Column L:** Direct cost component (labour + consumables + hand tools + scaffolding)
- **Column M:** Direct material component (material to be procured — as specified in SOW)

This distinction is very important: In some items, material procurement is from you (column M filled), in others material will be free-issued by the Contractor (column M empty or zero).

### 8.2 Coordination Risks

| Risk | Description | Impact |
|---|---|---|
| **Synchronization with civil works** | Excavation/backfill is civil's work but timing is yours | Earthing network delay risk |
| **Coordination with mechanical** | Heavy lifting (transformer, switchgear) from mechanical discipline | Crane sharing and timing |
| **Overlap with instrumentation** | Loop check, energization are shared work | Team synchronization |
| **Heat tracing/insulation** | Heating cable test must be done before insulation | Insulation crew waiting cost |
| **Pipe + electrical conduit** | Underground pipe and electrical conduit in the same trench | Sequencing and depth coordination |

### 8.3 Unit Price Traps

1. **"Any quantity, height, depth, distribution, congestion, complexity"** — Your unit price will be valid UNDER ALL CONDITIONS. Same price for 2 meters height and 40 meters height.

2. **Scaffolding embedded** — Scaffolding man-hours are included in each item's unit price but scaffold height coefficients increase from 10m onward (1.10-1.70). Difficult to give an accurate price without knowing the average height.

3. **Two sites, one price** — U58 and SS 00-BA-815 sites may be different but the same unit price will be applied.

4. **Transformer size ranges** — Price by kVA/MVA range but there can be significant cost differences between the smallest and largest transformer within a range.

5. **Cable termination count** — 1 cable = 2 ends = 2 separate payments. But while termination difficulty increases proportionally with cross-section, per-unit payment is fixed.

### 10.2 Pricing Strategy

- **Earthing (1610):** High volume, low unit price — productivity is key
- **Substation (1620):** Low quantity, high unit price — test duration is the dominant cost
- **Cabling (1630):** Highest volume item — waste allowance and scaffold factor are critical
- **Heat tracing (1650):** Specialist work — coordination cost is high
- **Lighting (1680):** Can be high volume depending on fixture count — do not forget intermediate cabling is included
- **Testing (1690):** Not paid separately! Add 8-15% test time to each item
