## 1. PROJECT SUMMARY AND PURPOSE

SOCAR (State Oil Company of Azerbaijan Republic) is relocating its refinery operations from Baku city center to Sumgayit due to environmental pollution. The new refinery will produce fuel to Euro-5 standards, process the same crude oil quality and quantity as HAOR Refinery Phase 2, and operate in integration with the Azerikimya Steam Cracker Unit (ASCU).

Two crude oil blends will be processed: Light Crude Mix (LCM) and High Sulfur Crude Mix (HSCM — Short Kero and Long Kero scenarios).

**Main Contractor:** Technip Energies Italy S.p.A., Rome
**Client/Owner:** SOCAR
**Location:** Sumgayit, Azerbaijan

### 3.2 PRICE STRUCTURE EXPECTATION

Technip Energies' price structure consists of two main components:

#### A. UNIT PRICE PART (UP) — Unit Price
There is a separate Price Schedule (PS) file for each discipline. Based on unit price x estimated quantity. If quantity changes, unit price remains fixed; payment is based on actual quantity.

#### B. LUMP SUM PART (LS) — Lump Sum
In SP04/SP05 packages, buildings are priced entirely on a lump sum basis. A separate lump sum price will be given for each building.

**Final price formula:** `Total = UP + LS + V (Variation)`

#### Indirect Cost Adjustment Mechanism
Indirect cost is automatically adjusted based on deviation of direct costs from estimates:
- Deviation <= 15% → Indirect remains unchanged (Y=1)
- Deviation > 15% → Graduated multiplier applied (multipliers between 0.6-0.9)

This mechanism is very important: If work volume drops more than 15% below estimate, your indirect costs are also cut. If it increases more than 15%, you receive partial compensation instead of full increase.

### 3.9 CONTROLLED EXPENSES (HOURS/DAYS FOR VARIATION)

Prices to be applied in case of Variation are predetermined:

**Labour hourly rates (all-in):**
- Normal working hours: Declared hourly rate
- Overtime (06:00-22:00): K0 = **1.15** multiplier
- Night work (22:00-06:00): K1 = **1.30** multiplier
- Sunday/public holiday: K2 = **1.65** multiplier
- Multipliers are not applied cumulatively

**Equipment rental:**
- Daily rate = Monthly rate / 26
- Hourly rate = Daily rate / 10
- Downtime is not paid
- Obligation to bring equivalent equipment in case of serious breakdown

**Scaffolding:**
- Volume calculation: h x w x L
- Height coefficients: up to 10m = 1.00, 10-20m = 1.10, 20-30m = 1.25, 30-40m = 1.40, above 40m = **1.70**
- Enclosed area/inside tank: **2.00** multiplier
- Rental period: From delivery date to dismantling permit

**Stand-by rates:**
- Labour: **80%** of normal rate (i.e., 20% discount)
- Equipment: **60%** of normal rate (i.e., 40% discount)

### 4.3 CLAUSES IN FAVOR OF THE CONTRACTOR

1. **"All-inclusive" principle:** Any work not explicitly assigned to the Contractor in the SOW is the Subcontractor's responsibility. This creates a very broad scope risk.
2. **Price is fixed and final:** No escalation, exchange rate adjustment, or any other correction will be made.
3. **Unit price applies under all conditions:** "Any quantity, height, depth, at any distribution, congestion, or complexity" — meaning unit prices do not change even under difficult conditions.
4. **Design errors are your responsibility:** "No ground claims based on defective description or differences between final drawings and relevant documents."
5. **Site knowledge assumed:** By participating in the tender, you accept that you have perfect knowledge of the site.
6. **Scaffolding double burden:** Scaffolding man-hours must be embedded in each item code's unit price BUT indirect scaffolding cost must also be declared separately. If not calculated carefully, there is a risk of double-counting or under-counting.

## 6. CRITICAL ASSESSMENT AS AN ELECTRICAL ENGINEER

### 6.1 Items Requiring Special Attention in Electrical Scope

1. **Energization procedure (MS-2093-02):** Electrical energization tests are very critical. Separate energization procedures will be applied to substations, MCC, switchgear, and UPS systems. A separate method statement must be prepared for each.

2. **Solo Motor Run (MS-1693-01):** Individual running test for all motors. It is impossible to accurately calculate this cost without knowing the motor count — reviewing SOW detail documents (inside ZIP) is essential.

3. **Loop Check (MS-1593-01):** Loop test from DCS, ESD, PLC systems to each local instrument. This is an instrumentation discipline task, but the electrical team is directly involved in providing power.

4. **Building electrical systems (SP04/SP05):** Lump sum price includes electrical distribution, lighting, earthing, lightning, cable trays, telecommunication, fire detection. Providing lump sum without BOQ is high risk — drawings must be reviewed.

5. **Unit price including scaffolding:** Scaffolding man-hours must be embedded in unit prices for work performed at height, such as cable pulling, tray installation, and lighting installation. Height factors (from 1.10 above 10m to 1.70) must be calculated.

6. **Ex-proof (ATEX) areas:** Refinery environment — there will be explosive atmosphere zones. Ex-proof equipment installation and certification is an additional cost.

### 6.2 Critical Electrical Documents Expected from ZIP Files

- **SOW-1600-0000:** Electrical discipline detailed scope of work — this file is of key importance
- **PS-1600-xxxx:** Electrical Price Schedule — unit price structure and item codes
- **PD-1600-xxxx:** Electrical Price Description — measurement conventions and definitions
- **CSD attachments:** Electrical drawings (single line diagrams, cable schedules, layout drawings)
- **SOW-1500-0000:** Instrumentation SOW (for loop check electrical coordination)
